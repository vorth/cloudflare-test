## {{ title }}

<p>This can be done either by capturing the butterfly, taking a picture of it, or recognizing it. There are many online resources that can help you quickly identify butterfly species by color pattern.</p>

<p><span class="full-image-block ssNonEditable"><img style="width: 500px;" src="{{ global.imagesUrl }}/storage/butterfly_families2.jpg" alt="" /></span></p>

<p>The most comprehensive website for North American butterflies is <a href="http://www.butterfliesofamerica.com">Butterflies of America</a>. This site reviews butterflies by family and contains photographs of live adults, pinned specimens, immatures, habitat, larval hostplants, and bibliographies.</p>

<p>At the same time, if you're not terribly familiar with butterflies nor understand the basic appearance and differences of these 7 butterfly families, you may want to check out these resources that show a comprehensive listof butterfly species by thumbnails; so that you can quickly scan these images to find your species of butterfly.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.naba.org/chapters/nabast/GalleryVisualKey.html">North American Butterfly Association Image Gallery</a></p>
