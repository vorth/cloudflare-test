    <h2>Techniques for Obtaining Immatures</h2>
<p>This section will discuss&nbsp;the disciplines of&nbsp;how to locate&nbsp;butterfly immatures in the wild as well as&nbsp;how to get live female butterflies to lay eggs in captivity.&nbsp;&nbsp; Understanding some fundamental principles can help you get started.&nbsp;</p>

<td width="559">
<p><strong>Step 1:&nbsp; Find the habitat for a particular species of butterfly.&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>The key to finding caterpillars is first understanding&nbsp;the habitat&nbsp;for&nbsp;the adult butterfly.&nbsp; There are many types of butterfly habitat.&nbsp; The common element to these habitats usually is the availability of&nbsp;plants that feed both the adult butterflies as well as their caterpillars.&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="/habitat/">More.</a></p>
<p><strong>Step 2: Identify the species of butterfly.</strong></p>
<p>This can be done either by capturing the butterfly, recognizing it,&nbsp;or taking a picture of it. There are many online resources that can&nbsp;help you quickly identify buttefly species by color&nbsp;pattern. &nbsp;<a href="/butterfly-identification/">More.</a></p>
<p><strong>Step 3: Identify the species of plant(s) caterpillars of this butterfly use in your habitat.</strong></p>
<p>Now that you know the species of butterfly, you have several resources you can&nbsp;research to learn what species of plants--termed larval host plants--you need to check out to find caterpillars.&nbsp; The <a href="/hostplant-identification/">Host Plant Identification</a> section arranges documented larval hostplants as associated to butterfly familes.&nbsp; Another great resource is <a href="http://www.butterfliesofamerica.com">Butterflies of America</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Step 4: Return to your habitat and&nbsp;look for eggs or caterpillars on their&nbsp;larval host plant.</strong></p>
<p>Once you've identified the species of larval host plant associated with your butterfly, return to the habitat and search&nbsp;for this plant.&nbsp; Next, look for eggs or caterpillars either on the leaves or flowers.&nbsp;&nbsp;There is an encyclopedia of knowledge with regards to understanding&nbsp;helpful hints to help you find&nbsp; caterpillars of specific butterflies on their respective host plants.&nbsp; <a href="/finding-immatures/">More.</a></p>
<p><strong>Step 5: Return to your habitat to collect live females to set up for laying eggs in the lab.</strong></p>
<p>If you're having a hard time finding butterfly eggs or caterpillars on their larval hostplants for whatever reason,&nbsp;consider collecting and caging live females to get them to lay eggs.&nbsp;&nbsp; <a href="/female-oviposition/">More.</a></p>
